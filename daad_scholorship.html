DAAD Scholarships

Various Universities, Germany

Fully Funded

About the Scholarship

The DAAD (Deutscher Akademischer Austauschdienst) is the German Academic Exchange Service, offering funding for international students to study or research in Germany at various degree levels.

DAAD scholarships are awarded to highly qualified graduate students, PhD candidates, and postdocs from developing and newly industrialized countries for study and research at German universities.

Scholarship Benefits

  • Monthly stipend (€850 for graduates, €1,200 for doctoral candidates)
  • Payments towards health, accident, and personal liability insurance
  • Travel allowance (unless covered by home country)
  • Tuition fees (in some cases)
  • Monthly rent subsidy and family allowances (where applicable)

Key Details

Eligible Degrees:

Master's, PhD, Postdoc, and Research Grants

Duration:

10-24 months for Master's, up to 4 years for PhD

Application Deadline:

Varies by program (typically August-October annually)

Number of Awards:

Over 100,000 scholars supported annually worldwide

Eligibility Criteria

  • Applicants from developing and newly industrialized countries
  • Bachelor's degree (usually a four-year program) completed within last 6 years
  • For Master's: Minimum 2-3 years of professional experience
  • For PhD: Master's degree completed within last 6 years
  • Strong academic record and research proposal (for research grants)
  • German or English language proficiency (depending on program)
  • Not eligible if you've been in Germany for more than 15 months at application deadline
  • Clear demonstration of motivation and future plans to contribute to home country's development

Application Process

1

Find the Right Program

DAAD offers many different scholarship programs. Use their scholarship database to find the one that matches your qualifications and goals.

2

Prepare Application Documents

Typically includes: DAAD application form, CV, motivation letter, research proposal (if applicable), academic transcripts, language certificates, letters of recommendation.

3

Submit Application

Applications are typically submitted through the DAAD portal or through German embassies/DAAD offices in your home country.

4

Selection Process

Applications are reviewed by independent selection committees. Shortlisted candidates may be invited for interviews.

Tips for Successful Application

Research Proposal

For research grants, clearly outline your research objectives, methodology, and how your work will benefit your home country.

Motivation Letter

Explain why you chose Germany, your specific program, and how this aligns with your career goals and home country's needs.

Language Preparation

Even for English programs, basic German skills strengthen your application and help with daily life in Germany.

University Contacts

For research grants, demonstrate contact with potential supervisors at German universities.
