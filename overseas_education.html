Exams Required for Overseas Study

Depending on your chosen country, program, and level of study, you may need to take one or more of these standardized tests.

English Proficiency Tests (IELTS, TOEFL, PTE, Duolingo) ▼

These tests assess your ability to read, write, listen, and speak in English. They are mandatory for non-native English speakers applying to universities in English-speaking countries.

  • IELTS (International English Language Testing System): Widely accepted globally, especially in the UK, Australia, Canada, and New Zealand. Scores are from 0-9 band. Most universities require 6.0-7.5.
  • TOEFL (Test of English as a Foreign Language): Very popular in the USA and Canada, but also widely accepted elsewhere. Scores from 0-120. Most universities require 80-100+.
  • PTE Academic (Pearson Test of English): A computer-based test gaining popularity, accepted in many countries including the UK, USA, Canada, and Australia. Scores from 10-90.
  • Duolingo English Test: An accessible online test accepted by an increasing number of universities, especially for flexibility. Scores from 10-160.
Graduate Level Exams (GRE, GMAT) ▼

These aptitude tests are typically required for admission to postgraduate programs (Master's, PhD) in various fields.

  • GRE (Graduate Record Examinations): Required for most Master's and PhD programs, particularly in the USA and Canada, across diverse fields like engineering, sciences, arts, and humanities. Assesses Verbal Reasoning, Quantitative Reasoning, and Analytical Writing.
  • GMAT (Graduate Management Admission Test): Primarily required for MBA and other business-related Master's programs worldwide. Focuses on Analytical Writing, Integrated Reasoning, Quantitative Reasoning, and Verbal Reasoning.
Undergraduate Level Exams (SAT, ACT) ▼

These are standardized admission tests for undergraduate programs, predominantly in the USA.

  • SAT (Scholastic Assessment Test): Measures skills in Evidence-Based Reading and Writing, and Math.
  • ACT (American College Testing): Assesses skills in English, Math, Reading, and Science, with an optional Writing section.

Understanding Overseas Education Costs

The total cost of studying abroad comprises tuition fees and living expenses, which vary significantly by country, city, and university.

Average Annual Costs (Approximate Ranges)

USA

  • Tuition: $25,000 - $55,000 USD (per year)
  • Living Costs: $10,000 - $18,000 USD (per year)
  • *Larger cities generally higher costs.

United Kingdom

  • Tuition: £12,000 - £35,000 GBP (per year)
  • Living Costs: £10,000 - £15,000 GBP (per year)
  • *London costs significantly higher.

Canada

  • Tuition: CAD $15,000 - $35,000 CAD (per year)
  • Living Costs: CAD $10,000 - $15,000 CAD (per year)

Australia

  • Tuition: AUD $20,000 - $45,000 AUD (per year)
  • Living Costs: AUD $15,000 - $22,000 AUD (per year)

Germany (Public)

  • Tuition: Often free/minimal (Semester Contribution €100-€350)
  • Living Costs: €10,000 - €12,000 EUR (per year)
  • *Most programs require German language.

Ireland

  • Tuition: €10,000 - €25,000 EUR (per year)
  • Living Costs: €7,000 - €12,000 EUR (per year)

Note: These are average estimates. Actual costs may vary. Scholarships and financial aid are available to help manage expenses.

Eligibility & Document Checklist

General Eligibility Criteria

  • Academic Qualification: Minimum 10+2 for Bachelor's, Bachelor's degree for Master's/PhD. Specific GPA/percentage requirements vary by university and country.
  • English Language Proficiency: Required scores in IELTS/TOEFL/PTE/Duolingo (see Exams section).
  • Standardized Test Scores: GRE/GMAT for graduate, SAT/ACT for undergraduate (if applicable).
  • Financial Capability: Proof of sufficient funds to cover tuition and living expenses for at least the first year.
  • Age: Generally, 18+ for university admissions, but check specific program requirements.
  • Relevant Experience: For some postgraduate or specialized programs, work experience or a portfolio may be required.

Key Documents Required

  • Academic Transcripts: Mark sheets and degrees from all previous educational institutions.
  • Passport: Valid passport with sufficient validity (usually 6 months beyond intended stay).
  • Statement of Purpose (SOP) / Personal Statement: A compelling essay outlining your academic goals, reasons for choosing the program/university, and future aspirations.
  • Letters of Recommendation (LORs): From professors or employers, typically 2-3.
  • CV/Resume: Highlighting academic achievements, work experience, and extracurricular activities.
  • English Proficiency Test Scores: (IELTS, TOEFL, PTE, Duolingo) official score reports.
  • Standardized Test Scores: (GRE, GMAT, SAT, ACT) official score reports.
  • Financial Documents: Bank statements, loan sanction letters, scholarship letters, sponsor letters proving funds.
  • Passport-size Photographs: Recent, as per specific country/visa requirements.
  • Application Fee Payment Receipts.
  • Any other country/university-specific documents.
